  1. History



    1. Background

      2. Cricket, a bat-and-ball game played between two teams of eleven players, originated in England in the 16 th century. It evolved from informal games to a more structured sport, becoming England's national game in the 18 th century

    2. Foundation

      3. The game involves eleven players per team, with one team batting to score runs while the other bowls and fields. Cricket matches are typically played on a large field with a central pitch, and can be enjoyed in various formats, including Test matches, one-day games, and the shorter Twenty20 format

    3. Expansions and termination

      4. Cricket is currently undergoing expansion, particularly in the United States with the emergence of Major League Cricket (MLC). However, this expansion is also facing challenges, including contractual disputes and power struggles between USA Cricket and private consortiums like American Cricket Enterprises (ACE).

  3. Organisation



    1. Tournament format

      2. Cricket tournament formats vary, but generally include limited overs (like One Day Internationals (ODIs) and Twenty20s (T20s)) and multi-day matches (like First-Class cricket). Limited overs matches are completed in a single day, with teams batting for a set number of overs (e.g., 50 in ODIs, 20 in T20s).

    2. Player acquisition, squad composition and salaries

      3. In IPL,player acquisition is primarily through auctions, though teams can also retain players from previous seasons. Squad composition involves a mix of retained players, those bought at auction, and sometimes trades. Salaries are based on the auction price, with variations for retained players and potential performance-based bonuses.

    3. Match rules

      4. The standard rules of cricket are significantly adapted to allow visually impaired players to participate in the sport, which is known as Blind Cricket. These adaptations primarily focus on making the game audible and tactile, as well as adjusting certain rules to account for the limitations of visual impairment

    4. Prize money

      5. The 13th edition of the ICC Men’s Cricket World Cup will see the winners of the 10-team tournament earn US$4 million, along with the trophy that they will lift at the Narendra Modi Stadium in Ahmedabad on 19 November.
